1. Headline: OPC Now Has an Official “National Standard”

China’s First AI-OPC Terminology Standard

On July 9, China released its first terminology-type group standard focused on the AI One-Person Company field. It draws a clear boundary for a concept that has been hot for a full year.

Official definition: An AI OPC is a company controlled and led by one core individual, generally with no more than 10 employees, whose main business is the research, development, application, or service of AI technology. The core individual treats personal brand and professional capability as core assets, and achieves independent operation and scalable delivery through AI agents.

Notably, from the April draft to the final release, the key change is that the OPC definition tightened from “no more than 3 shareholders and 20 employees” to “1 person in control, no more than 10 people.” Regulators are emphasizing the essence — a core decision-maker plus AI productivity — rather than a simple micro-team.

Tencent’s WorkBuddy OPC Community Lands in Chengdu

On July 10, the country’s first Tencent WorkBuddy OPC cooperative community was established in the Tianfu Changdao Digital Cultural & Creative Park in Chengdu High-Tech Zone. Takeaway: When a tech giant directly builds OPC communities, the track has moved from “grassroots” to “platform infrastructure.” For solo founders, the barriers of tools, compute, and traffic will keep falling.

4. Business Models: How Does an OPC Actually Make Money?

Combining the report with real cases, the mainstream monetization paths fall into a few buckets:

1. Custom AI agents for SMEs — The most direct cash-flow path. Many SMEs need AI but can’t afford a team. Build them customer-service agents, sales assistants, content tools, and data pipelines. Projects range from a few thousand to tens of thousands of yuan.

2. General agent products on subscription — Build standardized products for vertical audiences (e-commerce sellers, creators, sales reps) sold monthly. High upfront cost, but passive income once it works.

3. AI content entrepreneurship / IP traffic — Use agents to solve low content-output efficiency, turning creators from executors into “process commanders.”

4. Deep-industry expert “external brain” — Founders with strong industry backgrounds provide highly specialized consulting, compliance, or supply-chain optimization as an OPC.

A recent interview by China Informatization Weekly nailed a key point: most people see OPC’s flexibility and freedom but ignore the operating logic, capability moat, and real-world boundaries behind it. Three additions:

  • AI is leverage, not the engine. AI executes, but direction, judgment, and taste still depend on the human. Without a strong core, AI just helps you do the wrong things faster.
  • Standardization = replicable = replaceable. A 4,000-yuan barrier means others can enter easily too. The real moat is your personal brand, industry insight, and trust assets.
  • For long-term survival, don’t just chase subsidies and hype. Getting one customer to pay continuously is the fundamental basis for an OPC to survive.

What’s scarce are those who can compound their use of AI and turn personal brand into an asset. The tools are democratized; the rest comes down to who has the sharper mind and stronger execution.
