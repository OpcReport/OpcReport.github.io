1. Definition and Essence of OPC

🎯 What is OPC (One-Person Company)?

OPC (One-Person Company) refers to a micro limited liability company that relies on AI tools and automation systems, where a single founder completes the entire workflow of product design, R&D, operations, and marketing. Its core characteristics are:

  • Team Size: Generally no more than 5 people (Hainan policy definition), typical configuration is 1 person + AI toolchain
  • Tech Leverage: AI Agents handle 70%+ of repetitive tasks, founder focuses on core decisions
  • Legal Entity: Has independent legal person status, enjoys limited liability protection of a limited company
  • Business Model: Light assets, high margins, scalable

The Essence of OPC

The essence of OPC is the extreme extension of individual capabilities through tech leverage. As the slogan of the popular GitHub project OneClaw states: “One person. An AI army. A fully operational company.”

OPC ≠ Traditional Solo Entrepreneurship
Traditional solo entrepreneurs rely on their own skills and time. OPC entrepreneurs use AI Agents as “digital employees,” achieving 10x-100x leverage.

🔥 Driving Force 1: Rise of Chinese AI Models, AI Tool Costs Plummet

In 2026, Chinese AI models (DeepSeek, Tongyi Qianwen, Kimi, Zhipu Qingyan, etc.) have caught up to or even surpassed some international models in Chinese understanding and localization scenarios, with prices only 1/10 of the latter. An OPC can now obtain productivity that previously required a 10-person team at a cost of less than 500 RMB per month.

Key Chinese AI Models (2026):

  • DeepSeek: Open-source, cost-effective, strong reasoning ability
  • Tongyi Qianwen (Alibaba): Multimodal, strong in e-commerce scenarios

Stanford HAI’s 2026 annual prediction points out that AI has entered the “Evaluation Era,” and AI Agents are evolving from “you ask, it answers” to “you state goals, it executes autonomously.”

3. OPC Entrepreneurship Landscape: From MVP to Million-Dollar Revenue

📌 Model 1: AI Application Rapid Validation (Lowest Barrier)

💡 Case Study: French Indie Developer Marc Lou’s “Rapid Trial-and-Error Method”

Achievement: 2025 annual revenue $1.03 million (approx. 7.4 million RMB), all from products developed by one person, no funding, no team, no office.

Methodology:

  • Launched 21 products in 2 years, 12 achieved profitability
  • Core philosophy: “Don’t pursue perfection, ship when it runs, rapid trial-and-error, continuous iteration”
  • Flagship product ShipFast (monthly revenue $20,300): NextJS boilerplate code tool helping developers quickly build SaaS applications
  • Tech stack: Claude Code + Cursor + GitHub Copilot, 95% of codebases AI-generated (Y Combinator W25 data)

Inspiration for OPC Entrepreneurs:
Don’t pursue “big and comprehensive,” pursue “fast and accurate.” Use AI tools to compress the cycle from idea to launch to within 7 days.

📌 Model 2: Skill IP Model (Most Sustainable)

In 2026, the entrepreneurship logic has shifted from “information products” to “cognitive rental.” AI can instantly generate standard answers, making generic knowledge no longer valuable. What users are willing to pay for is your real-time judgment on specific problems, your industry insights.

Minimum Viable Entrepreneurship Model (Triangular Stable Structure):

  1. First Corner: Hard Skills — You must have a “scalpel” (e.g., legal consulting, fitness coaching, industry analysis)
  2. Second Corner: AI System — Your “one-person army” (use AI to capture trends, build frameworks, generate materials)
  3. Third Corner: Personal IP — Your trust amplifier (trusted in specific domains, not pursuing viral hits)

📌 Model 3: Vertical Niche Account + AI Content Factory (Easiest to Replicate)

Broad content niches are already saturated, but vertical domains still have plenty of gaps. You don’t need millions of followers, you only need 1,000 precise followers.

Success Cases:

  • Shenzhen AI Manga Team: Used Jimeng AI for storyboards + Jianying AI for editing, 23 episodes cost 12,000 RMB, one-month launch with 100M+ views, platform revenue 400,000 RMB+ (ROI 1:33)
  • AI Digital Human Parenting Blogger: Monthly affiliate commission 46,000 RMB, neither appears on camera nor raises children
  • Insurance Vertical Account: AI-generated content + precise traffic, annual revenue tens of millions RMB

5. 2026 H2 OPC Trend Predictions

🔮 Trend 1: AI Agent Evaluation Era Arrives

Stanford HAI points out that in 2026, AI will transition from the “Evangelism Era” to the “Evaluation Era.” Enterprises will no longer ask “Should we adopt AI?” but “What’s the ROI of this Agent?”

Impact on OPC: AI Agents will shift from “fun to play with” to “truly useful.” Agents with real business metrics, central management, and traceable value will win. OPC entrepreneurs need to learn to quantify the value of AI tools.

🔮 Trend 2: World Model Becomes AGI Consensus Direction

The training objective of large models is upgrading from “guess the next word (Next Token Prediction)” to “predict the next state (Next-State Prediction),” marking the rise of World Models.

Impact on OPC: AI will possess stronger causal reasoning capabilities, no longer a “statistical parrot” but a “digital employee” that understands the laws of the physical world. OPC can use World Models for more complex decision support (e.g., market prediction, user behavior simulation).

🔮 Trend 3: Embodied Intelligence Moves from Demo to Factory

Humanoid robots will break through Demos in 2026 and transition to real industrial and service scenarios. Embodied intelligence is moving from lab demonstrations to industrial screening and deployment.

Impact on OPC: The opportunity is not in “building robots,” but in “selling shovels” — embodied data will become the hottest AI entrepreneurship direction. OPC can focus on:

  • High-quality training data labeling and generation
  • AI algorithm optimization for robot application scenarios
  • “Middleware” tools for embodied intelligence (similar to “robot apps” for mobile phones)

🔮 Trend 4: From “Solo Fighting” to “Ecosystem Symbiosis”

OPC does not mean “working behind closed doors.” 2026 will see more OPC-exclusive ecosystem platforms:

  • OpenCSG × WAIC OPC Pioneer Challenge: The world’s first top-tier孵化 platform specifically built for super individuals and AI entrepreneurs
  • Moxun Tech SoloFounder AI Entrepreneurship Coaching System: 7-step actionable process to transform personal skills into mature business models
  • CSGLite + CSGClaw: Providing startup tools for OPC, AgenticHub and CSGHub providing complete product support for organizational OPCs

6. OPC Entrepreneurship Pitfalls Guide: 2026 Practical Advice

⚠️ Pitfall 1: Believing in “Full Automation,” Ignoring “Human-AI Collaboration”

Upwork November 2025 research points out that the success rate of Agents completing tasks independently is still not high — but the success rate of human-AI collaboration is significantly higher. Don’t treat AI as a “black box,” treat it as a “copilot.”

Advice: OPC workflows should be “AI drafts → Human reviews → AI optimizes → Final delivery,” not “AI fully automated.”

⚠️ Pitfall 2: Pursuing “Big and Comprehensive,” Ignoring “Small and Beautiful”

Many OPC entrepreneurs want to build “the next ByteDance” right from the start, resulting in scattered resources and slow progress. The opportunity in 2026 lies in ultra-vertical domains.

Advice: Use “nano-level segmentation” thinking to choose your track. Not “AI education,” but “AI teaching middle-aged and elderly people to do health exercises”; not “AI writing,” but “AI helping small businesses with tax and financial reports.”

⚠️ Pitfall 3: Ignoring “Compliance Risks,” Planting a “Time Bomb”

Although OPC is a “one-person company,” the legal liability is that of a “limited company.” If AI-generated content involves copyright, privacy, false advertising, etc., the founder may bear joint liability.

Advice:

  • When using AI-generated content, be sure to conduct manual review
  • If involving user data, comply with the “Personal Information Protection Law”
  • AI-generated content should be labeled “AI-assisted creation”
  • Purchase “cybersecurity liability insurance” (annual fee approx. 2,000-5,000 RMB)

7. Conclusion and Action Plan

🎯 Core Conclusions of 2026 OPC Entrepreneurship

  1. OPC has transitioned from “concept” to “business reality” — The rise of Chinese AI models + maturation of AI Agent technology + release of policy dividends, the combination of the three makes “one person is an army” possible.

  2. The key to success is not “tech stacking,” but “precise positioning” — Choose an ultra-vertical track, use AI tools to maximize efficiency, and use personal IP to build a trust moat.

  3. The opportunities in H2 2026 lie in “evaluation,” “World Model,” “embodied data,” and “ecosystem platforms” — Layout these directions in advance, and you will gain “time-difference dividends.”

  4. OPC is not “fighting alone,” but “partnering with AI” — Learn to use human-AI collaboration models to replace fully automated fantasies, and use ecosystem symbiosis thinking to replace working behind closed doors.
