The OPC Era: Truth Behind the Data

If you’ve been seeing “OPC” frequently online lately, don’t be surprised—2026 is the year this concept truly lands.

Key Statistics

  • As of June 2025, one-person limited liability companies in China exceeded 16 million
  • New registrations in H1 2025 reached 2.86 million, a 47% year-over-year increase
  • One-person companies account for 23.8% of all newly registered enterprises
  • Globally, over 1/3 of new companies are founded by solo founders, growing 53% in six years

OPC (One Person Company) advocates “one person operating a small, beautiful individual business.” What truly transformed it from concept to executable model is the maturity of AI tools from 2024-2026. By comprehensively using AI agents, automation toolchains, and cloud services, individual entrepreneurs can build and operate micro-enterprises covering the entire business chain with unprecedented efficiency.

Three Golden Rules for Choosing Your Track

Rule 1: Choose a Sector with “Structural Advantage”

Use AI to amplify your existing advantages, not to replace capabilities you don’t have. If you understand law, build legal AI tools; if you understand healthcare, build medical data analysis. Purely relying on “using AI to produce content” is no longer enough—you need something others can’t replace: industry experience, network resources, deep cognition.

Rule 2: Choose a “Short Monetization Path”—Survive First

Many people start wanting to build “vertical SaaS” or “AI platforms”—monetization cycles of 6-12 months, burning through resources before earning.

Recommended path:

  • 0-3 months: Choose the shortest monetization path, secure your first paid order
  • 3-6 months: After stabilizing income, consider higher unit-price directions
  • 6+ months: Productize, scale

Rule 3: One-Person Company’s Moat is “Professional Depth,” Not “Doing Everything”

After AI, content, code, and design production costs approach zero. Purely relying on “using AI to produce content” is insufficient. Small teams’ core competitiveness is never scale and resources, but decision agility and low delivery costs.

What Sectors Are Absolutely NOT Suitable for Small Teams?

  • ❌ General Assistant Track: Already monopolized by giants with computing clusters, data flywheels, and ecosystem barriers
  • ❌ Pure API Wrappers: No deep customization, no industry Know-How,随时 vulnerable to platform disintermediation

What Sectors Suit Small Teams?

  • ✅ Vertical Scenarios + Strong Workflow Embedding: Requiring “Industry Know-How + Non-standard Data Processing” dual capabilities
  • ✅ Enterprise Internal Pain Points: “Legacy code integration,” “Non-standard SOP implementation,” “Industry-specific rule adaptation”—scenarios giants won’t descend to and standardized products can’t cover

Pitfall Guide: OPC’s Real Challenges

Business Registration & Finance

  • One-person company registration differs from regular companies
  • Financial compliance requirements are stricter
  • Tax planning needs professional guidance

Supply Chain Collaboration

  • Single point of failure risk is high
  • Need reliable partner networks
  • Technical debt accumulates easily

Psychological Aspects

  • Loneliness is real
  • Decision pressure falls entirely on one person
  • Work-life boundaries blur

Outlook: What’s Next for OPC

From Beijing Zhongguancun to Wuhan Optics Valley, to Shanghai Lingang, over 12 million individual entrepreneurs nationwide are pouring into this track. One person, one computer, one AI system—independently completing the entire business chain from product development and content creation to market deployment.

However, when the “single-person army” slogan echoes through venture capital circles, we need to clear the fog of frenzy and see the underlying logic and real reefs of this new business species.

“AI flattened technical barriers but amplified action gaps.”

Those truly successful OPC entrepreneurs aren’t people who simply rely on AI tools, but those who understand how to use AI to amplify their core advantages and build genuine professional barriers in a niche field.

Conclusion

The One Person Company revolution is here. Whether you’re a developer looking to break free from corporate constraints, a domain expert seeking to monetize your expertise, or an aspiring entrepreneur testing the waters—2026 presents unprecedented opportunities.

The tools are ready. The market is primed. The question is: Are you ready to become a one-person company?
