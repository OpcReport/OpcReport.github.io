🔭 Trend Analysis: Where is OPC Heading in H2 2026?

1. From “Tool Frenzy” to “Business Frenzy”: Monetization is the Name of the Game

H1 2026 saw the OPC track transition from concept hype to real execution — but the market has already seen its first bifurcation. Data shows the OPC business survival rate is only 12.4%, with the vast majority failing at the same hurdle: “I have AI tools, but I can’t find customers.” The real competition in H2 will center on vertical scenarios and commercial closed loops.

2. Policy Dividend Window: Apply Early

29 provinces have issued 100+ specific policies. But policy resources are scarce. Historical patterns suggest early-stage policies tend to be loose and permissive, while later stages tighten standards. Act now — identify your local policy package and apply before the window narrows.

3. AI Agents: From “Assistant” to “Collaborator” — Where’s the Next Breakout?

Current mainstream AI agents have achieved the complete loop: Task Decomposition → Tool Invocation → Autonomous Execution → Result Verification — processing daily tasks equivalent to 3-5 full-time employees at under 1/10th the cost. But this is just the beginning. The next breakout point is multi-agent collaboration systems — AI agents with different specialties working together like virtual teams.

4. Global Opportunity: Chinese OPC Going Overseas is Ripe

With Chinese LLMs (Alibaba Qwen, Xiaomi MiMo, Zhipu GLM-5V-Turbo) gaining strength in Chinese language understanding and localized scenarios, and platforms like OpenClaw lowering full-stack development barriers, Chinese OPC entrepreneurs are facing an unprecedented overseas window. AI manhua, AI digital employees, smart hardware — on these tracks, Chinese teams’ iteration speed leads globally.

Best for: All OPC entrepreneurs
