1. Policy & Standards: OPC Finally Gets Its “Official ID”

🏛️ China’s First AI-OPC Terminology Standard Published — Effective Aug 1

Led by the Zhejiang Digital Economy Development Center and the Zhejiang Promotion Association for Intelligent Economy & Smart Cities, with 14 co-drafting organizations, the 《Artificial Intelligence OPC Terminology》 group standard was officially released and will take effect on August 1, 2026. It is the country’s first terminology standard focused on the AI-OPC domain, defining 27 new terms to characterize the “super individual.”

Core definition: An AI-OPC is a company dominated and controlled by one core natural person, with generally no more than 10 employees, whose main business is AI technology R&D, development, application, or services. The core individual treats personal brand and professional capability as core assets, leveraging intelligent agents to achieve independent operation and scaled delivery. The boundary was tightened from the April draft (“≤3 shareholders, ≤20 employees”) to “1 person in command, ≤10 employees.”

5. Trend Analysis

  • From “wild growth” to “by the book”: The AI-OPC terminology standard turns OPC from a folk buzzword into a formally bounded business form, favoring compliance, financing, and government-procurement alignment.
  • “1 person in command + N AI digital employees + ∞ global collaboration” becomes the standard architecture: The minimum viable unit of entrepreneurship compresses from “team” to “individual”; the toolchain (agent harness, low-code, automation) is the core lever.
  • Policy, capital, and platforms fire together: 5% tax + multiple rent-free funds + bank toolboxes + a $320M AHE round + WAIC/Global-Digital-Economy-Conference zones signal a maturing infrastructure.
  • Export and Agent-ization are the deterministic directions: The three converging trends — globalization, OPC-ization, Agent-ization — plus mature open-source agent projects give indie devs a low-cost starting point.

6. Risk Notes

  • Moat is not “using AI,” it’s “professional depth”: As content/code/design production costs approach zero, raw AI output is no longer enough; industry experience, network, and deep insight are the real barriers.
  • Avoid the general-assistant track: With compute clusters, data flywheels, and ecosystem moats, solo/small teams building general agents risk becoming “also-rans” — focus on narrow verticals with strong workflow embedding.
  • Beware the “engineering trap”: Falling LLM API costs make hand-rolling an agent look zero-threshold, but ambiguous SOPs lead to tech-stack sprawl and stalled projects — define the standard operating procedure first.
  • Compliance and tax boundaries: Despite friendly tax rates, one-person companies must keep finances standardized and respect limited-liability boundaries to avoid personal-asset commingling risk.
