2. Monetization Pathway Panorama: From Zero-Barrier to Advanced Strategies

🚀 Zero-Barrier Tracks (Quick Start)

1. AI Data Annotation

  • Barrier: Lowest (basic computer skills required)
  • Work Content: Image annotation, speech transcription, text classification, sentiment annotation
  • Real Income: Hourly rate ¥30-50, ¥120-200 for 4 hours daily
  • Assessment: Zero barrier but obvious ceiling, suitable for those needing stable income with patience

2. AI Layout and Design

  • Barrier: Ability to use Canva or WPS
  • Services:
    • WeChat article layout: ¥20-100/article
    • PPT production: ¥5-20/page
    • Resume formatting: ¥50-150/document
  • Recommended Tools: Canva, WPS AI Layout, Meitu AI, Gaoding Design

3. AI Avatar Customization

Real Case: Started at ¥9.9 on Xianyu, exploded in the third week. One buyer posted work on Xiaohongshu, bringing orders for two consecutive weeks. Peak day: 23 orders, monthly net profit over ¥7,000.

Key Segments:

  • Pet anthropomorphism: ¥30-50
  • Couple commemorative portraits: ¥50-100
  • Baby 100-day avatars: word-of-mouth through mom communities

Insight: Customers aren’t buying technology—they’re buying “this image makes me happy.”

💡 Intermediate Tracks (Requires Skill Accumulation)

1. AI WeChat Article Ghostwriting

  • Income Range: ¥3,000-20,000/month
  • Efficiency Boost: 5-10x improvement using ChatGPT + Claude, scaling from ¥500/article to ¥2,000/article
  • Key Points:
    • Choose vertical domains
    • Inject personal insights after AI generates drafts
    • Accumulate case studies to raise prices
    • Maintain 2-3 long-term clients for stable income

⚠️ Warning: Never directly copy AI output—readers can tell immediately. Clients pay for “warm content,” not “machine-generated text.”

2. AI Short Video Commentary

  • Income Range: ¥5,000-30,000/month
  • Workflow: ChatGPT writes script → Jianying produces video → AI voiceover
  • Critical Success Factor: Time advantage—discover new formats one week earlier, validate workflow three days faster

3. AI Music Creation & Copyright Monetization

Emerging High-Value Track:

Real Data: According to a leading music library platform, AI-generated music copyright licensing revenue grew over 300% YoY in 2025. A single high-quality AI music track can generate monthly passive income of ¥2,000-8,000.

🎯 Advanced Tracks (Technology + Business Depth)

1. Vertical Domain AI Agents

Build automated workflows for specific professional groups (lawyers, HR, independent designers). Don’t build generic chatbots—create “colleagues who can start working immediately.”

Success Cases:

  • LegalBrief.ai: Lawyers upload PDF contracts → automatically flag risky clauses + generate revision suggestions
  • HireFlow: HR inputs job JD → automatically screens resumes, generates interview outlines, rates match scores

Launch Steps:

  1. Find 3 target users and understand 3 tasks they repeat weekly
  2. Build basic workflow with LangChain
  3. Deploy web UI with Vercel
  4. Pricing model: $19/month or $199/year

Recommended Tools: LlamaIndex (document processing), Ollama (local models), Supabase (user management)

2. AI Agent Deployment Services

Market Demand: Popular AI agents like OpenClaw are powerful but have installation/configuration barriers for non-technical users.

Monetization Methods:

  • Remote installation: ¥100-200/session
  • On-site service: ¥400-500

Real Case: 27-year-old Lei Zhenpeng from Wuhan generated ¥100,000 in one week by teaching clients how to “raise lobsters” (building AI digital employees).

  • Two Major Enterprise Implementation Challenges: Multi-agent collaborative governance, agent observation and optimization
  • Alibaba Cloud Solutions: Launched AgentTeams and AgentLoop platforms

  1. Multi-Agent Collaboration Becoming Mainstream: Transition from single agents to agent cluster collaboration

The 2026 market has already eliminated “script-style Agents.”

6. Pitfall Guide: Three Major Traps for One-Person Companies

Trap 1: Chasing Trends Instead of Pain Points

❌ Wrong Approach: Seeing “AI video is hot” and jumping into AI video without knowing who to serve or what problem to solve.

✅ Correct Approach: Find “willing-to-pay scenarios.” Why does pet anthropomorphism make money? Because owners feel happy seeing it—¥30 per image isn’t expensive at all.

Trap 2: Technology-Driven Instead of Demand-Driven

❌ Wrong Approach: Spending 3 months developing a “cool” AI product that no one buys.

✅ Correct Approach: First, find 3 target users and understand 3 tasks they repeat weekly, then start development. Keep MVP cycle within 3-5 days.

Trap 3: Greedy for Scale Instead of Single-Point Breakthrough

❌ Wrong Approach: Wanting to build “vertical SaaS” or “AI platform” from the start—6-12 month monetization cycle, running out of money before seeing income.

✅ Correct Path:

  • Months 0-3: Choose the shortest monetization path, complete first paid order
  • Months 3-6: After stable income, consider higher customer value directions
  • After 6 months: Productize and scale

8. Action Recommendations

If You’re a Zero-Experience Beginner:

  1. Start with AI data annotation or AI layout design to quickly earn first income
  2. Invest 2-4 hours daily to accumulate tool usage experience and client cases
  3. After 3 months, transition to higher customer value services (e.g., AI ghostwriting, AI avatar customization)

If You Have Technical Background:

  1. Choose vertical domain agent direction, deeply cultivate specific industry scenarios
  2. Use open-source models like Llama 3 to reduce costs, quickly validate MVP
  3. Build private knowledge bases (RAG) and tool-calling capabilities to construct moats

If You Have Industry Experience:

  1. Productize industry Know-How, provide AI + vertical domain consulting services
  2. Develop industry-specific agents or workflow automation tools
  3. Build personal brand through content output to attract targeted clients

The AI-powered one-person company revolution is no longer a future vision—it’s happening now. Key trends to watch:

  1. Agent-as-a-Service (AaaS): Specialized agents becoming the new “employees” for one-person businesses
  2. Micro-SaaS Proliferation: Tiny, focused tools solving specific problems for specific audiences
  3. Creator Economy 3.0: Individual creators leveraging AI to build media empires
  4. Decentralized Workforce: Geographic boundaries continuing to dissolve
