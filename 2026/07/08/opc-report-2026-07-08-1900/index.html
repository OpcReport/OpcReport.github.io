📖 Deep Analysis — Why Are 90% of OPCs Failing?

🔍 Three Core Reasons for Failure

Failure #1: Unlimited Responsibility Cognitive Bias

Many founders misunderstand the essence of OPC — they believe “doing everything alone” is the spirit of one-person companies. This ignores the hard ceiling of personal energy. Without AI toolchains, external resources, or co-founders to share the load, solo founders burn out on operations instead of focusing on product and market.

Failure #2: Fuzzy Skill-to-Revenue Pathways

52.7% of OPCs earn less than 7,000 CNY/month. The core issue: they have skills but no monetization path, ideas but no implementation method. Most entrepreneurs build products before validating user demand — starting from “what customers will pay for” rather than “what they want to build.” This is a fatal reversal of priorities.

Failure #3: Blind Toolchain Building

AI tools are dazzling, but more expensive is not always better. Successful one-person companies typically have highly customized toolchains (research AI, content AI, sales AI, customer service AI) rather than chasing the most feature-rich solutions.

💡 The Right OPC Mindset: You are the sole decision-maker, but through AI and toolchains, you possess “micro-team capabilities.” Delegate “roles” to AI — research AI for industry analysis, content AI for multi-platform drafts, sales AI for personalized outreach, and customer service AI for FAQ knowledge bases.
