Over the past week, the One-Person Company (OPC) model has graduated from “individual experiment” to “national strategy.” China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) and six other ministries explicitly called for “accelerating the cultivation of AI One-Person Companies (AIOPC)”; more than 20 cities have rolled out dedicated support policies; and Shenzhen, Suzhou, and Guangdong are putting real money on the table. 1. Policy Wind: AIOPC Enters National Documents

MIIT + six ministries: “accelerate cultivating AI One-Person Companies.”
On June 18, MIIT and six other departments released the Action Plan for Promoting Coordinated Development of Large, Small and Medium Enterprises in the Platform Economy (2026–2028), marking the first time a national ministry-level document explicitly calls for cultivating AIOPC. Mainstream outlets including People’s Daily Online and National Business Daily have followed up.

NDRC unveils five AI “15th Five-Year Plan” work streams.
On July 7, the NDRC’s Department of Innovation and High-Tech Development outlined five priorities for the 15th Five-Year Plan: faster indigenous innovation, stronger application pull, deeper ecosystem coordination, open-win cooperation, and safety/controllability. Shanghai, Jiangsu, and Guangdong have folded OPC into their 15th Five-Year Plans.

Four departments issue the “AI + Human Resources & Social Security” implementation opinion.
On July 8, the Ministry of Human Resources, NDRC, MIIT, and the National Data Administration released a document targeting 20 industry large-model application scenarios in 2026, exploring ~50 high-value scenarios by 2027, and universal adoption by 2030 — opening a clear track for OPCs to serve government/HRSS scenarios.

💡 Editor’s note: Policy density is the strongest signal of whether a track is “right on time.” When seven ministries co-issue a document and multiple provinces weave OPC into the 15th Five-Year Plan, the question shifts from “can I do this?” to “how will they subsidize me doing it?” For solo developers, now is the window to apply for compute vouchers, startup grants, and free workspaces.

5. Toolchain: The “Arsenal” for One Person = One Army

UXbot → Bolt → Cursor → Supabase → Stripe.
UXbot (requirement to deliverable front-end/App), Bolt (in-browser full-stack generation), Cursor (AI coding environment), Supabase (backend-as-a-service: DB/auth/realtime), Stripe (subscriptions & payments). Wired together, a solo founder can ship a SaaS MVP and monetize it.

💡 Editor’s note: A mature toolchain is the technical premise that lets an OPC “fight alone as an army.” But the moat is not “knowing how to use AI to produce” — it’s “industry Know-How + deep judgment.” Mass AI content production is no longer enough; professional depth is the real barrier.

6. Risks & Regulation: The Other Side of the Dividend

⚠️ AI anthropomorphism interaction rule effective July 15. The Cyberspace Administration, NDRC, MIIT, Ministry of Public Security, and SAMR jointly issued the Interim Measures for the Administration of AI Anthropomorphic Interaction Services — the world’s first dedicated regulation for AI anthropomorphic interaction, strengthening minor protection and drawing interaction boundaries. OPCs building digital humans / AI customer service / anthropomorphic products must get compliant early.

⚠️ Engineering traps. The general-assistant track is already monopolized by giants via compute, data flywheel, and ecosystem triple moats — individuals/small teams entering become “also-rans.” Solo founders fit better with “vertical scenario + strong workflow embedding” narrow Agents. Also beware of OPC hype — surviving (landing your first paid order) matters more than chasing the trend.

  • Go narrow, not wide: Focus on “vertical scenario + industry Know-How” AI Agents; avoid the general-assistant red ocean.
  • Monetize before scaling: Land the first paid order in 0–3 months, stabilize revenue in 3–6, then productize/scale after 6.
  • Compliance first: Self-audit anthropomorphic/digital-human/customer-service products before July 15.
  • Leverage the toolchain: Use UXbot + Bolt + Cursor + Supabase + Stripe to validate SaaS ideas fast and push “cost of doing” to the floor.
