1. Policy Spotlight: AI-OPC Enters National Strategic Layout

Ministry-Level Policy Breakthrough

On June 18, 2026, China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT), alongside the Cyberspace Administration of China, National Development and Reform Commission, and four other ministries, jointly issued the “Action Plan for Promoting Platform Economy Large-Medium-Small Enterprise Collaborative Development (2026–2028)” (MIIT Document No. 119/2026). This marks the first time AIOPC (AI-powered One-Person Company) has been included in a national policy document — elevating solo AI entrepreneurship from individual experiment to national strategic layout.

Source: Economic Daily / Ministry of Industry and Information Technology Official Document

20+ Cities Launch OPC Support Policies

As of June 2026, over 20 Chinese cities have introduced OPC-specific support policies. Shanghai, Jiangsu, Guangdong, and other provinces/cities have incorporated OPC into their “15th Five-Year Plan”.

Shanghai’s latest policy highlights:

  • Removed the restriction of one person registering only one OPC — same individual can now register multiple OPCs for business separation
  • OPC now operates on a subscription capital system with a maximum 5-year contribution period
  • No minimum registered capital requirement — startups typically register with 100,000–1,000,000 RMB

Source: 光明网 (Guangming Daily) / Shanghai Policy Compilation, July 2026
