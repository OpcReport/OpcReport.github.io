OPC in 2026: How Policy and Platforms Are Standardizing the One-Person Company Boom

OPC in 2026: How Policy and Platforms Are Standardizing the One-Person Company Boom

Web3 & AI Entrepreneurship Observation — July 13, 2026

The One-Person Company (OPC) is no longer just a buzzword. Over the past week, three signals confirmed that OPC is being pushed forward on a standardized, institutionalized, and policy-driven track: China released its first AI-OPC terminology standard, Tencent launched the country’s first WorkBuddy OPC cooperative community in Chengdu, and Hainan announced an 800-million-yuan reward pool for high-quality OPC communities. Here is the breakdown.

1. Headline: OPC Now Has an Official “National Standard”

China’s First AI-OPC Terminology Standard

On July 9, China released its first terminology-type group standard focused on the AI One-Person Company field. It draws a clear boundary for a concept that has been hot for a full year.

Official definition: An AI OPC is a company controlled and led by one core individual, generally with no more than 10 employees, whose main business is the research, development, application, or service of AI technology. The core individual treats personal brand and professional capability as core assets, and achieves independent operation and scalable delivery through AI agents.

Notably, from the April draft to the final release, the key change is that the OPC definition tightened from “no more than 3 shareholders and 20 employees” to “1 person in control, no more than 10 people.” Regulators are emphasizing the essence — a core decision-maker plus AI productivity — rather than a simple micro-team.

Tencent’s WorkBuddy OPC Community Lands in Chengdu

On July 10, the country’s first Tencent WorkBuddy OPC cooperative community was established in the Tianfu Changdao Digital Cultural & Creative Park in Chengdu High-Tech Zone. Jointly launched by Tencent Cloud and Chengdu Hi-Tech Wenchuang Media, it was also part of the 2026 APEC Digital Week city-side events.

  • Built on Tencent’s self-developed AI workbench WorkBuddy as the technical foundation
  • Explores a “government support + platform support + community co-creation” tripartite model
  • Aims to build a full-lifecycle OPC growth path, not just point subsidies

Takeaway: When a tech giant directly builds OPC communities, the track has moved from “grassroots” to “platform infrastructure.” For solo founders, the barriers of tools, compute, and traffic will keep falling.

2. Policy Map: A White-Hot “Talent + Money” Race

Hainan: 800 Million to Reward Top Communities

Leveraging its Free Trade Port status, Hainan issued “Measures to Support AI OPC Innovation and Development”: cultivate around 3 high-quality OPC ecosystem communities in 2026, and by 2028 aim for 10+ quality communities, 100+ quality OPC firms, and 1,000 innovators.

Shenzhen: Elevated to City Strategy

Earlier this year Shenzhen released its “Action Plan to Build a Leading AI OPC Entrepreneurship Ecosystem (2026-2027),” targeting a dozen-plus OPC communities and 10,000+ AI entrepreneurs by end of 2027 — the first time a city elevated the one-person company to strategic level.

Shanghai Lingang & the Yangtze River Delta

Shanghai Lingang’s “Super Individual 288” plan aims to build 10 OPC communities, open 100 real-world scenarios, and gather 1,000 teams and 10,000 creators within three years. Wuxi, Nanjing, Changzhou, and Kunshan are following closely.

⚠️ Note: Subsidies are aggressive (single-item support reaching the tens-of-millions-yuan level), but subsidies are icing, not the cake. Don’t start a business just to chase subsidies — validate your commercial loop first, then let policy dividends be a bonus.

3. The Numbers: How Big Is the Global OPC Pie?

Key figures from the iResearch/EO Intelligence “2026 Global AI OPC Business Insight Report”:

  • Existing stock: ~30.4M one-person companies in the US, ~12M in the EU, ~16M in China
  • Efficiency gain: Single-person output is 8-10x that of a traditional team
  • Startup barrier: Startup cost dropped to under 4,000 yuan
  • Gross margin: Commonly 50%-70% (some standardized SaaS/knowledge models approach 98%)
  • Capital entry: Domestic OPC equity financing exceeded 12 billion yuan in 2025

Cost Collapse Is the Root Driver

💡 Between 2024 and 2026, the operating cost of an equivalent-capability AI Agent fell 128x — from ~$5,000/month to under $40/month. This is the underlying logic that turned “an army of one” from a slogan into reality: when the marginal cost of a digital employee approaches zero, individual output can be scaled almost infinitely.

4. Business Models: How Does an OPC Actually Make Money?

Combining the report with real cases, the mainstream monetization paths fall into a few buckets:

1. Custom AI agents for SMEs — The most direct cash-flow path. Many SMEs need AI but can’t afford a team. Build them customer-service agents, sales assistants, content tools, and data pipelines. Projects range from a few thousand to tens of thousands of yuan.

2. General agent products on subscription — Build standardized products for vertical audiences (e-commerce sellers, creators, sales reps) sold monthly. High upfront cost, but passive income once it works.

3. AI content entrepreneurship / IP traffic — Use agents to solve low content-output efficiency, turning creators from executors into “process commanders.”

4. Deep-industry expert “external brain” — Founders with strong industry backgrounds provide highly specialized consulting, compliance, or supply-chain optimization as an OPC.

5. A Sober Take: Three Things to Get Straight

A recent interview by China Informatization Weekly nailed a key point: most people see OPC’s flexibility and freedom but ignore the operating logic, capability moat, and real-world boundaries behind it. Three additions:

  • AI is leverage, not the engine. AI executes, but direction, judgment, and taste still depend on the human. Without a strong core, AI just helps you do the wrong things faster.
  • Standardization = replicable = replaceable. A 4,000-yuan barrier means others can enter easily too. The real moat is your personal brand, industry insight, and trust assets.
  • For long-term survival, don’t just chase subsidies and hype. Getting one customer to pay continuously is the fundamental basis for an OPC to survive.

⚠️ Trend to watch: The report predicts OPC will shift from “AI-assisted” to “AI-native,” and produce “one-person unicorns” valued over $1 billion. Overseas cases like Lovable, Base44, and Golpo AI have already achieved rapid financing and monetization with ultra-lean teams. This path is being genuinely walked.

6. Bottom Line

In July 2026, OPC completed a critical leap from “concept” to a “standard + platform + policy” trinity. For ordinary people, this is a once-in-a-decade window — but windows never lack applicants. What’s scarce are those who can compound their use of AI and turn personal brand into an asset. The tools are democratized; the rest comes down to who has the sharper mind and stronger execution.
