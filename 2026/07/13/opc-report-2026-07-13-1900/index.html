🔮 Key Trend Deep Dives

Trend 1: AI Shifts from “Assistant Tool” → “Core Productivity”

AI agents have achieved a complete closed loop: Task Decomposition → Tool Calling → Autonomous Execution → Result Verification. One AI agent processes the equivalent workload of 3-5 full-time employees per day, at less than 1/10 the cost. Platforms like OpenClaw and Miaoda are enabling entrepreneurs to become “super individuals,” independently building commercial-grade applications across verticals like AI web series, smartwatches, AI+HR, and enterprise AI sites.

Trend 2: Cost Collapse — AI Democratization Accelerates

DeepSeek and other open-source models have driven AI costs down dramatically. Inference costs continue to fall; open-source models are rapidly closing the gap with frontier models. Domestic LLMs (Qwen, MiMo, GLM-5V-Turbo) have collectively upgraded, making powerful AI accessible to ordinary entrepreneurs. “One person + AI = one company” has transformed from concept to executable reality.

Trend 3: OPC Enters the “Capability Competition Phase”

As the OPC ecosystem matures and policies become more refined, the industry consensus is clear: running a commercial closed loop and maintaining stable cash flow is the true competitive moat. With a survival rate of only 12.4%, the market is shifting from “concept euphoria” to “capability screening.”

Trend 4: From “Solo Entrepreneurship” → “Network Collaboration”

The launch of China’s first WorkBuddy OPC Collaboration Community in Chengdu signals OPC’s evolution from “lone wolf” to “collaborative network.” Through platforms and communities, OPC entrepreneurs gain resource connections, government access, and AI tool support. The future will see massive 1-5 person micro-networked teams, with some becoming extraordinarily successful.
