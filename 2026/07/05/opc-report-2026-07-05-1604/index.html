1.2 OPC Core Definition & Business Logic

OPC (One Person Company) is redefined in the AI era: a new type of entrepreneurial organization where individuals, supported by AI tools, can relatively independently complete the full-chain business closed loop of product R&D, marketing, and user services.

Key Characteristics:

  • Light Asset: No need for large teams and office space; one person + AI tools can launch
  • High Agility: Extremely short decision chain, rapid response to market changes
  • Strong Focus: Specialize in vertical niche areas, creating “small but beautiful” profit models
  • Full Chain: AI undertakes the complete process from product design and R&D to market launch

1.3 Technology Driver: AI from “Auxiliary Tool” to “Digital Employee”

2026 is called the “Year of Digital Employees”. AI agents represented by OpenClaw have completely broken traditional code barriers:

  • Lowered Threshold: Non-tech “beginners” no longer need to learn programming languages; through natural language conversations, they can transform vague needs into operable business applications
  • 7×24 Hour Execution: AI evolves into a “virtual team” that never sleeps, undertaking repetitive tasks such as code writing, content creation, and customer service
  • Cost Plummet: Open-source models like DeepSeek have brought down AI usage costs, with inference costs continuously decreasing, allowing ordinary entrepreneurs to afford and effectively use AI tools

VI. Risk Warnings & Compliance Suggestions

⚠️ Important Reminder:
Although the OPC model lowers the entrepreneurship threshold, it does not mean "zero risk." Entrepreneurs need to pay attention to the following risks:
  1. Technology Dependency Risk: Over-reliance on a single AI platform may face risks such as service interruption and price adjustment. It is recommended to build multi-cloud/multi-tool backup solutions
  2. Data Security Compliance: Processing user data requires compliance with the “Personal Information Protection Law” and “Data Security Law” to avoid compliance risks
  3. Intellectual Property Protection: Copyright ownership of AI-generated content is still controversial. It is recommended to consult legal professionals
  4. Intensifying Market Competition: Lower OPC thresholds mean intensified competition, requiring continuous building of core competitiveness (such as personal brand, proprietary data, vertical field knowledge)
  5. Income Volatility Risk: OPC usually lacks stable cash flow, requiring reasonable planning of financial buffers
