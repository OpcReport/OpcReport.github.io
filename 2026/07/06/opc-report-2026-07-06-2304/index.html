Executive Summary

The One-Person Company (OPC / Solopreneur) model has moved from a buzzword to scaled, real-world adoption. Three signals are now appearing simultaneously:

  • Scale signal: On Sesame Credit’s enterprise platform alone, “verified” one-person companies (sole natural-person-owned) have reached nearly 5.7 million, up 42% YoY; technology promotion and software/information services together account for 17.9% of them, showing deep binding with the digital economy.
  • Capital signal: Silicon Valley now has accelerators built specifically for solo founders (Solo Founders Program), offering $100K founder-friendly investment; a solo project hitting $2M ARR in 4 months is already a real case.
  • Tooling signal: Big tech (Alibaba, Tencent, Microsoft) and the open-source community are simultaneously productizing “AI Agent productivity,” letting individuals run the full product–operations–support chain without a team.

Takeaway: 2026 is positioned by the industry as the “Year of the AI Agent.” The bottleneck for OPCs has shifted from “can the tech support it” to “can the individual design good task flows plus an external service network.”

Trend Analysis

  • From “going solo” to “minimally-teamed operation”: Real OPCs don’t mean one person doing everything — the individual designs task flows and runs the business via AI Agents + automation + external service networks (AI enlarges the manageable radius).
  • “Pick-and-shovel” plays heat up first: In embodied AI and agent infrastructure, the data/tool layer monetizes earliest (cf. Alibaba Databridge, Tencent efficiency agents).
  • Globalization + OPC-ization + Agent-ization are the three core trends; AI going global remains high-certainty given better overseas payment habits and softer competition.
  • Funding structure is shifting: Solo / tiny-team funding rounds are rising; accelerators now design terms for “no team.”

Action Tips for Solo Founders

  • Target a specific role’s specific task, not a general assistant; wrap AI into a ready-to-use lightweight SaaS with monthly billing.
  • Start from an existing Chinese MVP, then translate + localize (e.g., adapt to Western holiday templates) for overseas launch to lift conversion.
  • Leverage open-source frameworks (DeerFlow, oh-my-claudecode, one-person-company toolkits) to prototype fast and cut trial cost.
  • Get familiar with tools before they go paid (e.g., Databridge Agent before Aug billing) to capture the efficiency window.

Risk Notes

  • OPCs depend heavily on individual capacity and AI-tool stability — single-point failure risk is higher than team-based companies.
  • Policy and compute subsidies are regional and time-bound; re-test the profit model after subsidies fade.
  • Copyright, compliance, and platform rules for AI-generated content remain uncertain.
