V. Deep Observation: Is the Rise of OPC Inevitable or Hype?

💭 Analyzing OPC from Technical Architecture, Economic Models, and Organizational Behavior

Source: CSDN | Published: 2026-06-26

Concepts like Solopreneur, independent developers, and OPC (One-Person Company) have gradually moved from the edge terms of the tech circle to the center stage of business discussions.

The Underlying Logic of OPC’s Rise:

  1. Technical Feasibility: AI tools have matured enough to replace the core functions of traditional teams (market analysis, content creation, product development, customer service, etc.)
  2. Economic Rationality: AI inference costs have dropped by 95% in two years, making “one-person companies” economically fully feasible
  3. Organizational Superiority: OPC avoids pain points of traditional companies such as hierarchical communication costs, decision-making delays, and personnel management, achieving ultimate agility and efficiency

Limitations of OPC:

  1. Scale ceiling: Certain businesses requiring large-scale human collaboration (e.g., manufacturing, offline services) are difficult to achieve through OPC
  2. Risk concentration: All risks of OPC are borne by the founder alone, lacking team sharing
  3. Loneliness: Long-term solo entrepreneurship may lead to mental health problems; need to actively build social networks

Conclusion: OPC is not a “panacea,” but a highly promising new type of entrepreneurship model, especially suitable for knowledge-intensive, creativity-intensive, and technology-intensive niche fields.
