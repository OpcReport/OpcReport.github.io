💡 What is OPC?

OPC (One-Person Company), also known as “One-Person Company,” advocates “one-person operation, small but beautiful individual business.” What has transformed it from concept to executable model is precisely the maturity of AI tools during the 2024-2026 period.

Core Formula:

One person, one computer, one set of AI systems can independently complete the entire commercial closed loop from product R&D, content creation to market launch. AI is no longer merely a tool for writing copy or making posters, but has evolved into a “virtual team” that works 7×24 hours without rest.

III. OPC Monetization Models Full Analysis

Model 1: AI Content Services 📝

Content production is currently the easiest OPC monetization direction to enter.

Service Types:

  • AI short video production
  • AI graphic account operation
  • AI official account operation (monthly retainer)
  • AI live streaming copywriting
  • AI e-commerce detail pages
  • AI brand content marketing

Common Monetization Methods:

  • Per-piece pricing (AI short videos, graphics)
  • Monthly retainer (official accounts, brands)
  • Package pricing (e-commerce content)
  • Project-based pricing (live streaming scripts, brand planning)

Model 2: AI Agent Development 🤖

One of the most valuable skills in the future is helping enterprises build AI agents.

Enterprise Pain Points:

  • Low customer service efficiency
  • Slow content production
  • Employees don’t know how to use AI
  • Enterprise knowledge base chaos
  • SOP processes not standardized

OPC Solutions:

  • Build customer service agents
  • Build sales agents
  • Build knowledge base systems
  • Build AI workflows
  • Build marketing automation systems

Suitable Industries: E-commerce/education (customer service), ToB enterprises (sales assistants), SMEs (knowledge bases), operations teams (workflows), local businesses (marketing systems)

Model 3: AI Training and Education 📚

AI training is one of the fastest-growing markets. OPCs can monetize through knowledge payment, online courses, training camps, etc.

Protagonist: Wang Yubo (born in 1997)

VII. Challenges and Risks: OPC Is Not a Panacea

⚠️ Challenges That Must Be Faced

  1. Although technical barriers are lowered, product design capability requirements are higher

    AI can write code, but it can't write good product experiences. OPC entrepreneurs need stronger product thinking and user insight.

  2. Fierce market competition, differentiation is more difficult

    Everyone can use AI tools. How to achieve differentiation is key.

  3. Unstable income, need for diversified layout

    One-person companies have weak risk resistance and need to establish multiple income sources.

  4. Loneliness and decision pressure

    There's no one to consult with; all decisions must be made independently.

  5. Compliance and legal risks

    Tax, intellectual property, data privacy, and other issues need to be handled independently.

VIII. Future Outlook: The Next Explosion Points for OPC

📈 Trend Predictions for H2 2026

  1. OPC + SaaS: Small but Beautiful Tools in Vertical Fields

    • AI assistance tools targeting specific industries (lawyers, doctors, teachers) will explode.

  2. OPC + Hardware: Personal Branding of AI Wearable Devices

    • With AI, personal brands can launch their own smart hardware products (smartwatches, health monitoring devices).

  3. OPC + Content: AIGC-Driven Media Matrix

    • One person operating content matrices for dozens of accounts will become the norm.

  4. OPC + Education: Scaling of Knowledge Payment

    • AI reduces course production costs dramatically; individual teachers can serve global students.

  5. Continued Policy Support

    • More local governments are expected to introduce OPC support policies, forming a wave of “OPC entrepreneurship parks.”

IX. Advice for OPC Entrepreneurs

🎯 Action Checklist

  1. Choose the Right Track: Prioritize vertical fields you're familiar with or have resources in; avoid being too general.
  2. Tools First: Master at least 3-5 AI tools and establish your own toolchain.
  3. Quick Validation: Use MVP (Minimum Viable Product) thinking to quickly launch products and get user feedback.
  4. Build Personal Brand: Establish a professional image through content output (official accounts, short videos, social media).
  5. Join Communities: OPC is not a solo battle; join communities like OPC China to access resources and support.
  6. Compliant Operation: Understand tax, intellectual property, and other legal issues early to avoid future troubles.

X. Conclusion: The Era of Super Individuals Has Arrived

In 2026, OPC has transformed from concept to reality, from fringe to mainstream. The maturity of AI technology, cost reduction, and policy support have jointly driven this entrepreneurship wave.

One person, one computer, one set of AI tools can start a company. This is no longer science fiction, but a reality happening now.

For young people who want to start a business, OPC provides a low-risk, high-reward path. But it must also be clearly recognized that OPC is not a shortcut; it requires stronger comprehensive capabilities, clearer product thinking, and more resilient willpower.

The future is already here; it’s just not evenly distributed. Those entrepreneurs who embrace the OPC model first are redefining the boundaries of “company” and writing commercial legends belonging to super individuals.

One person equals a team.
In the AI era, everyone can be a super individual.
