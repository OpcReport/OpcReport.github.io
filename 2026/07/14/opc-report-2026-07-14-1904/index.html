1. Executive Summary

The “One-Person Company” (OPC) — a natural-person wholly-owned company supercharged by AI — has evolved from a niche concept in 2025 into a mainstream startup paradigm in 2026, propelled by the convergence of policy, capital, and tooling. AI Agents have upgraded from “assistive tools” to “digital employees,” and the model of a single person leveraging the output of a five-person team is being repeatedly validated across short-drama, e-commerce, cross-border trade, and music verticals. Meanwhile, Guangdong, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Shenzhen, and Wuhan continue to scale up incentives — compute vouchers, model vouchers, and equity co-investment now reach individual founders directly.

3. Tech Trends: AI Agents From “Tools” to “Digital Employees”

3.1 Three-Stage Evolution

  • 2025 — Human + Agent: A single Agent executes a specific task.
  • 2026 — Human + Agent Swarm: Multiple Agents collaborate across entire workflows (current mainstream).
  • 2027 — Human + Autonomous Company: Agents that self-decide, self-optimize operations, and respond to markets — the embryo of the “autonomous company.”

3.2 Domestic Agent Platform Landscape

  • Comprehensive: iFlytek AstronClaw (full-stack localized), 360 Zhiyu Agent Platform, Tencent Cloud Agent Platform.
  • Vertical: Shenzhixin (industrial), Hulingxi (financial risk control).
  • Open-source / lightweight: Dify, CrewAI — low-cost deployment for small teams.
  • DingTalk Wukong: Now a key SME AI-retrofit entry point, with frequent two-week full-deployment cases.

3.3 Market Size

2026 is called the “first year of AI Agent scaled deployment.” China’s enterprise Agent market is projected to reach ¥44.9B, growing 107% YoY, with on-premise deployment exceeding 60% of the mix. The AI Law mandates in-country private deployment for important data, making localization and custom development a hard necessity.

⚠️ Reality check: Is the OPC the biggest windfall or the biggest trap of 2026? The profitable OPCs cluster into three types — those with real domain know-how, those who deliver results, and those who catch the policy-dividend window. “PPT startups” with only information asymmetry and no execution will keep getting washed out. AI lowers execution cost, not decision cost.
