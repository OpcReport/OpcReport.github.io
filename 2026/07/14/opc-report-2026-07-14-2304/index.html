1. 🏛️ Top-Level Policy: National Strategy Accelerates, Cities Race to Compete

1.1 Seven Ministries Jointly Issue Document: AI OPC Incorporated into National Strategy

In June 2026, seven ministries including the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) jointly issued an official document explicitly proposing to “accelerate the cultivation of AI OPC.” This marks the first time AI OPC has been incorporated into the national policy framework — the “human + AI = company” entrepreneurship model is now officially recognized.

More than 20 cities nationwide have introduced OPC-specific support policies. Shanghai, Jiangsu, and Guangdong have included OPC in their “15th Five-Year Plan.”

1.2 Beijing Shijingshan: Up to ¥6M Per Category, Eight Support Measures

📌 Shijingshan OPC Policy (Released July 10, 2026) — Key Terms:

  • 🪙 ¥100,000 Startup Grant — One-time support for newly registered OPC enterprises
  • 🏠 3-Month Free Housing — Housing support for new OPC enterprises
  • ⚡ ¥2M Compute Subsidy — District-wide pooled compute resource allocation (not per-company; total pool across all OPC enterprises)
  • 🏆 Competition Awards — Up to ¥500,000 for award-winning projects
  • 🏢 Park Construction — Dedicated OPC entrepreneurship park
  • 💰 Financial Support — Financing matchmaking and guarantee subsidies

⚠️ Note: The ¥2M compute subsidy is a district-wide pool, not per-company. The ¥100,000 startup grant and housing support are per-category maximums. Other measures can stack up to ¥6M total.

1.3 Guangdong Province: 3-Year Plan to Become National OPC Hub

2. 🔥 Industry Dynamics: Ice and Fire in the OPC Wave

2.1 National Competition Launches Dedicated OPC Track

On July 3, 2026, the National AIGC Multimodal Innovation Design & Technology Application Competition (AI OPC Industry Group), jointly guided by the Ministry of Science and Technology, Ministry of Education, MIIT, and China Association for Science and Technology, was officially launched in Beijing. This is the first time a national-level competition platform has opened an independent track for “one-person companies.”

The organizer, Jinshang AI OPC Entrepreneurship Incubator Platform, simultaneously announced the launch of the “Million AI OPC Empowerment Project”, aimed at driving employment through entrepreneurship.

2.2 16M OPCs Nationwide, Survival Rate Below 15%

⚠️ Critical Data:

  • 2026 is dubbed the “Year One of OPC.” Nationwide OPC registrations have exceeded 16 million.
  • Real survival rate is only under 15% — over 13.6 million have ceased operations or become zombie companies.
  • 32 OPC enterprises are clustered in Beijing’s Zhongguancun AI North Latitude Community; average founder age under 30.
  • More than one-third of new companies are now founded by solo founders — a 53% surge in six years.

2.3 90% of Copycat Founders Eliminated: The OPC Gold Rush Fades

According to Kuaikeji (May 2026), the “one-person company” craze that swept 2026 is receding rapidly, with brutal reality emerging: over 90% of the copycat OPC registrants have already been eliminated. Main reasons include:

  • No clear monetization path — AI tools alone don’t create competitive advantage
  • Insufficient market research and user needs assessment — products with no buyers
  • Over-reliance on AI while neglecting personal judgment and resource integration
  • Operating costs (platform fees, compute fees, tool subscriptions) far exceeding expectations

2.4 WAIC × OpenCSG: OPC Independent Pioneer Challenge

The World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) and OpenCSG jointly launched the OPC Independent Pioneer Challenge, positioning it as the world’s first top-tier incubation platform exclusively for super-individuals and AI entrepreneurs.

Sam Altman’s prophecy is materializing: “In the AI era, it is possible for one person to build a billion-dollar unicorn.”

2.5 Digital Human & AI Music: New Content Creation OPC Track

Token Yuan Shen (Beijing) Technology Co. showcased digital human generation and AI music as a new direction for content creation OPCs, enabling users to obtain exclusive works through a one-stop digital platform.

3. 🛠️ Tools & Infrastructure: AI Stack as Core Competitive Advantage

3.1 FlagOS with Qwen3-8B Image Launches on Alibaba Cloud

In the OPC wave, the AI infrastructure stack is emerging as a key competitive differentiator. Open-source AI system software stack FlagOS supports multiple heterogeneous AI accelerator chips and provides a complete solution from public cloud APIs to private inference deployments — now officially available on Alibaba Cloud, giving OPCs more infrastructure choices.

3.2 AI Agent Daily Throughput = 3–5 Full-Time Employees, at 1/10 the Cost

Since 2025, AI agents have achieved a complete closed loop: task decomposition → tool invocation → autonomous execution → result verification. One AI agent’s daily task throughput equals 3–5 full-time employees, at less than 1/10 the cost of traditional human labor.

3.3 Case Study: Beijing FutureTech Tidal Technology

“My ‘AI employees’ handle solution coordination, code writing, code review, automated cloud server deployment, real-time information gathering, and investment advice. I only need to ‘command’ them to deliver standardized services.”
— Liu Yefei, Founder, Beijing FutureTech Tidal Technology Co., Ltd.

With 18 years of technology architecture and management experience, Liu Yefei founded his company with 5 “AI employees.” This year, he launched an AI Digital Employee Engineering Delivery Platform that helps SMEs build proprietary digital employees and enables experts to create digital avatars.

4. 💰 Funding & Monetization: Favorable Tax Policy, Innovative Models Emerging

4.1 SME Income Tax Effective Rate Drops to 5%

After the comprehensive deregulation of the new Company Law, one person can now establish unlimited “one-person companies” and even “subsidiary one-person companies” — the capital fission chain is fully unlocked. For small-scale micro enterprises, the effective tax rate on profits under ¥3M has dropped to 5%. Industry insiders view this as the state using an extremely broad moat to protect super-individuals.

5.2 Lessons from Bento Shutdown

The shutdown of once-highly-anticipated Bento platform proves again: for OPCs, tool selection and monetization capability matter more than passion. Blindly chasing new tools while neglecting the construction of a business closed loop is the root cause of many OPC failures.

7. 💡 Conclusion

The second half of 2026 is seeing the OPC track undergo a painful transition from “mania” to “rationality.” Out of 16 million OPCs, 85% will fade away — this is not the failure of entrepreneurs, but a necessary self-correction of the market.

The survivors will be super-individuals with vertical expertise, clear monetization paths, and genuine AI tool mastery. Policy bonuses are accelerators, not life rafts.

“AI tools make ‘one person, one army’ possible — but whether that ‘army’ can win the battle still depends on who that ‘person’ is.”
