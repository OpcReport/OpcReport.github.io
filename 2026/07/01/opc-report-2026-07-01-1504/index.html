1. 🏛️ Hainan Province Passes China’s First Provincial OPC Policy — Up to ¥8M in Incentives

On July 1, reports from Caixin and multiple outlets confirmed that Hainan Province’s Department of Industry and Information Technology issued the “Several Measures to Support the Innovative Development of AI OPC”. This is China’s first provincial-level policy package specifically targeting One-Person Companies (OPC).

Key Highlights:

  • Creates a “Super Founder Cluster Zone” with OPC community spaces ≥3,000㎡
  • Provides rent-free maker spaces for qualifying OPCs
  • Annual selection of 1–3 premium OPC communities — provincial finance provides up to ¥8 million in one-time rewards
  • Signals the transition of OPC from grassroots experimentation to formal policy recognition

💡 Impact: This is expected to trigger a wave of follow-up policies from other provinces. The banking sector is watching closely — small/medium banks are already piloting OPC financial services, while major banks remain cautious due to immature credit assessment systems.

Published July 1: Several employers who previously laid off workers citing AI replacement capabilities are now rehiring employees to restore team capacity and drive business growth. These companies discovered AI cannot cover full workflows — especially in business expansion, client communication, cross-departmental collaboration, and complex judgment calls.

Implication for OPC founders: Pure AI replacement has a ceiling. The “Human + AI” hybrid model is the sustainable path forward. OPC is not “zero humans” — it’s “one human + an AI employee team.”
