1. OPC Model Definition and Classification

1.1 What is OPC?

OPC (One-Person Company) refers to a one-person company, with the core proposition of “one person operating, small but beautiful individual business.” With the support of AI technology, individual entrepreneurs can independently operate a complete business closed loop — from product development and marketing to customer service, achieving intelligent management of the entire chain.

💡 Essential Restructuring: An OPC is never about one person doing all the work, but about one person commanding a system. This is not a motivational quote; it’s a business reality that is happening right now.

1.2 Three Major OPC Model Classifications

  1. Model Innovation Type: Utilizing AI to重构 (restructure) traditional business processes and create new business models (e.g., AI-powered content media matrix)

  2. Scenario Application Type: Focusing on specific vertical scenarios and providing AI-enabled solutions (e.g., AI code review tools)

  3. Ecosystem Service Type: Providing tools, training, community, and other ecosystem services for other OPCs (e.g., OPCBASE platform)

5. OPC Entrepreneurship Path Guide

5.1 Six Practical Paths

  1. AI Tool Development (SaaS Products) - Use AI to solve real pain points you encounter daily and create subscription-based SaaS products

  2. AI-Powered Content Media Matrix - Use AI to generate articles, videos, and social media content, monetizing through ads, affiliate marketing, and knowledge payments

  3. AI-Assisted Outsourcing - Accept orders through platforms like Upwork and Fiverr, using AI to improve delivery efficiency and quality

  4. AI Agent Custom Services - Customize AI agents for enterprise clients, solving specific business scenario problems

  5. Small App Development and Sales - Develop small utility applications and sell them through app stores or independent websites

  6. “Selling Shovels” in Vertical Scenarios - Enter the OPC ecosystem and provide tools, training, and consulting services for other super individuals

5.2 Pitfall Avoidance Guide

❌ Avoid: General Assistants

The general assistant track has been monopolized by leading giants, forming a closed loop. The triple advantages of computing power clusters, data flywheels, and ecosystem barriers are definitely not something individuals or small teams can break through in the short term. Entering this track means becoming a “companion runner.”

✅ Recommended: Vertical Scenarios + Strong Workflow Embedding

Focus on niche areas that require the dual capabilities of “industry Know-How (professional experience) + non-standard data processing,” such as:

  • Legal AI: Contract review, legal consulting
  • Medical AI: Assisted diagnosis, medical record analysis
  • Financial AI: Risk control models, investment analysis
  • Educational AI: Personalized tutoring, homework grading

7. Actionable Recommendations

  1. Find Real Pain Points: Start from problems you encounter daily; don’t dream up demands from thin air
  2. Quickly Validate MVP: Use AI tools to create a demonstrable version within 2 weeks and quickly test market response
  3. Focus on Vertical Scenarios: Go narrow and deep; don’t try to serve everyone from the start
  4. Establish a Payment Closed Loop: Set up payment points early to validate users’ willingness to pay
